# RossOS — Complete Role Matrix, Duties, AI Integration & Cross-Role Linking

> Every person touching a project should open RossOS and immediately know what they need to do today, what's blocking them, what they're blocking, and what changed since they last looked. No phone calls. No emails. No "did you see my text?" — the platform IS the communication.

---

## Core Philosophy

1. **Running To-Do Lists for Everyone** — Every role has a personalized, auto-generated task queue that updates in real-time based on project state, dependencies, and AI prioritization.
2. **Zero Ambiguity** — No one should ever wonder "what's the status of X?" — the answer is always visible, always current.
3. **Upstream/Downstream Awareness** — When you complete a task, the next person in the chain is automatically notified and their task becomes actionable. When you're delayed, everyone downstream knows instantly.
4. **AI as the Invisible Project Coordinator** — AI monitors everything, connects dots humans miss, predicts problems before they happen, and auto-generates the communication that would otherwise require meetings, calls, and emails.

## INTERNAL ROLES

### 1. Owner / Principal

**Who they are:** Company owner, final decision-maker, signs the checks, lands the clients, sets the vision.

**Daily duties:**
- Review company-wide financial health (cash flow, AR/AP, profitability by job)
- Approve large expenditures, change orders above threshold, draw submissions
- Client relationship management (high-touch, especially during sales and close)
- Strategic decisions (which jobs to bid, staffing, growth)
- Review and sign contracts, lien waivers, insurance docs
- Monitor all active projects at a glance
- Business development — networking, referrals, repeat clients

**How AI + software makes this effortless:**
- **Morning Briefing Dashboard** — AI-generated summary: "3 jobs on track, 1 needs attention (Smith job framing behind 2 days — sub has been notified, adjusted completion still within target). Cash position: $245K. Two invoices need your approval ($18K combined). Draw #4 ready for your signature."
- **Approval Queue** — Single screen showing everything waiting on you. One-tap approve/reject with AI-provided context ("This CO is 12% above estimate — similar COs on past jobs averaged 8%. Recommend reviewing scope.")
- **Cash Flow Forecasting** — AI projects cash position 30/60/90 days out based on scheduled draws, committed POs, pending invoices, and historical payment patterns. Alerts before shortfalls.
- **Client Sentiment Tracking** — AI analyzes client portal activity, selection response times, and communication patterns. Flags clients who may be frustrated before they call you.
- **Profitability Radar** — Real-time margin tracking per job with AI comparison to similar completed jobs. "Wilson job trending 3% below target margin — difference is electrical sub ($8K over) and lumber (+6% since estimate."
- **Smart Notifications** — Only bothered for things that actually need you. Everything else is handled by the team with AI guardrails.

**Links to other roles:**
- Receives approval requests from → PM, Office Manager, Superintendent
- Financial summaries from → Bookkeeper
- Client escalations from → PM, Client Portal
- Sales pipeline from → Marketing, Estimator
- Signs off on → Contracts, Draws, Large COs, Lien Waivers

### 2. Office Manager / Admin

**Who they are:** The operational hub. Keeps the office running, manages paperwork flow, handles phones/emails (until RossOS eliminates that need), coordinates between field and office.

**Daily duties:**
- Process incoming mail, invoices, insurance certificates, permits
- Route documents to correct people
- Maintain filing systems (digital and physical)
- Coordinate meetings, site visits, inspections
- Answer client questions about billing, timelines
- Track insurance certificates, licenses, warranties
- Onboard new vendors (W-9, insurance, tax info)
- Manage office supplies, equipment, subscriptions

**How AI + software makes this effortless:**
- **Intelligent Document Inbox** — Every document entering the system (email attachment, scan, photo, fax) is auto-classified by AI: "Invoice from ABC Electric → matched to PO-0142 on Smith job → routed to PM for approval → coded to 26000-Electrical." Zero manual sorting.
- **Auto-Vendor Onboarding** — Vendor submits info through portal. AI extracts W-9 data, validates EIN, checks insurance dates, flags gaps. Admin just reviews and clicks approve.
- **Insurance Tracking Dashboard** — AI monitors every COI expiration across all vendors. Auto-sends renewal requests 30 days out. Flags expired coverage before that vendor can be scheduled on any job.
- **Smart Scheduling Assistant** — Coordinates inspection scheduling with city/county availability, weather, and job readiness. "Framing inspection can be scheduled Thursday — all prerequisites complete, weather clear, inspector available 9-11am."
- **Client Communication Templates** — AI drafts responses to common client questions using current project data. "Hi Sarah, your selections for master bath fixtures are due by Feb 28. Here's what's been chosen so far and what's remaining. Jake will follow up if you'd like to discuss options."
- **Running Task List** — Auto-populated: documents to process, insurance certificates expiring this week, vendor onboarding in progress, inspection scheduling needed.

**Links to other roles:**
- Routes documents to → PM, Bookkeeper, Owner
- Insurance tracking for → Vendors/Subs, Superintendent
- Scheduling coordination with → Superintendent, Inspectors, Clients
- Vendor onboarding feeds → PM, Purchasing, Bookkeeper
- Receives requests from → Everyone (this role is the hub)

### 3. Project Manager (PM)

**Who they are:** Owns the job from contract to close. Responsible for budget, schedule, quality, and client satisfaction on their assigned projects.

**Daily duties:**
- Review job status across all assigned projects
- Manage budgets — track costs vs. estimates, approve POs and invoices
- Maintain and update project schedules
- Coordinate with superintendent on field progress
- Communicate with clients on progress, selections, changes
- Process and negotiate change orders
- Review and approve subcontractor scopes and bids
- Manage draw requests and lender requirements
- Track submittals, RFIs, and design clarifications
- Resolve issues escalated from field
- Weekly job meetings (internal + client)

**How AI + software makes this effortless:**
- **Job Command Center** — Single dashboard per job: budget health, schedule status, open items count, client activity, next milestones. AI highlights what needs attention NOW vs. what's on track.
- **Budget Variance Alerts** — AI monitors every cost code in real-time. "Framing labor is 18% over budget with 70% of work complete. Projected overrun: $4,200. Recommend reviewing remaining scope with superintendent." Alerts before overruns, not after.
- **Smart Schedule Management** — Gantt chart that auto-adjusts based on actual progress, weather forecasts, vendor confirmations, and inspection results. AI suggests re-sequencing when delays occur. "If we move drywall to start in Unit B while waiting for Unit A electrical rough, we save 3 days on critical path."
- **Change Order Generator** — AI drafts COs from conversation notes or field observations. Auto-calculates cost impact using estimate database, markup, and historical pricing. Routes through approval chain automatically.
- **Client Portal Intelligence** — See exactly what the client sees. AI tracks their engagement: "Client viewed budget summary 3 times this week but hasn't approved selections. Consider reaching out." Auto-sends progress updates with photos from daily logs.
- **Draw Request Builder** — AI compiles draw package: progress photos matched to line items, budget vs. actual, stored materials documentation, lien waiver status. One-click generate and send to lender.
- **RFI/Submittal Tracking** — Auto-generated from field questions, design conflicts, or AI-detected issues. Routes to architect/engineer with full context. Tracks response time and follows up automatically.
- **Running Task List** — AI-prioritized: "1. Approve Wilson PO (blocking framing start Monday). 2. Review Smith CO #3 (client waiting). 3. Schedule Johnson pre-con meeting. 4. Draw #5 due to lender Friday."

**Links to other roles:**
- Receives field updates from → Superintendent, Field Crew
- Budget data from → Bookkeeper, Estimator
- Design clarifications from → Architect, Designer, Engineer
- Client communication through → Client Portal
- Approvals to/from → Owner (large items), Superintendent (field decisions)
- Vendor management with → Purchasing, Vendors/Subs
- Financial reporting to → Owner, Bookkeeper

### 4. Superintendent / Site Supervisor

**Who they are:** Owns the field. Manages day-to-day construction activities, coordinates subcontractors on site, ensures quality and safety, and is the eyes and ears of the project.

**Daily duties:**
- Walk jobsite(s), assess progress, identify issues
- Coordinate subcontractor schedules and sequencing
- Complete daily logs (weather, crew counts, work performed, issues)
- Conduct quality inspections at each phase
- Manage material deliveries and staging
- Ensure safety compliance
- Request and schedule inspections
- Resolve field conflicts (design vs. reality)
- Communicate progress and issues to PM
- Manage punch lists during closeout
- Verify subcontractor work for payment

**How AI + software makes this effortless:**
- **Voice-to-Text Daily Logs** — Walk the site, talk into your phone: "Framing crew has 6 guys today, finished second floor walls, starting roof trusses tomorrow. Plumber rough-in complete in master bath. Issue: electrical panel location conflicts with HVAC chase — need RFI to architect." AI transcribes, categorizes, tags photos, creates the RFI, and updates the schedule.
- **Smart Site Coordination Board** — Visual daily view: which subs are on site today, what work is scheduled, what inspections are coming, what deliveries are arriving. AI flags conflicts: "Drywall delivery scheduled same day as trim crew start — staging area conflict."
- **Photo Documentation AI** — Take photos, AI auto-categorizes: pre-cover, progress, issue, safety. Tags by location (room/floor), trade, and date. Automatically attached to relevant daily log, inspection, or punch item.
- **Quality Checklist Engine** — Phase-specific digital checklists that know what trade work should be complete before the next phase. AI pre-populates based on plans and flags items that weren't checked off from previous phase.
- **Inspection Readiness** — AI tracks all prerequisites for each inspection type. "Framing inspection ready: shear nailing complete ✓, hold-downs installed ✓, hurricane clips ✓, fire blocking ✓. Missing: window flashing at 2nd floor east elevation." Prevents failed inspections.
- **Running Task List** — "1. Walk Smith job roof trusses (crew arriving 7am). 2. Meet plumbing inspector at Johnson 10am. 3. Verify Miller punch list items (3 remaining). 4. Review tomorrow's delivery schedule."

**Links to other roles:**
- Daily updates to → PM (auto-generated from logs)
- Schedule coordination with → PM, Vendors/Subs
- Quality sign-offs to → PM, Client (via portal)
- Safety reporting to → Safety Officer (if exists), Owner
- RFIs generated to → Architect, Engineer (through PM)
- Inspection requests to → City/County Inspectors
- Material requests to → Purchasing, Suppliers
- Punch lists shared with → Subs, PM, Client

### 5. Estimator

**Who they are:** Prices out projects before contract. Builds detailed cost breakdowns from plans, specs, and site conditions. Their accuracy determines profitability.

**Daily duties:**
- Review plans and specifications for new projects
- Perform quantity takeoffs (material counts from drawings)
- Solicit and compare subcontractor/vendor bids
- Build detailed cost estimates with labor, material, equipment, overhead
- Analyze historical cost data from completed projects
- Prepare estimate summaries and presentations for clients
- Update cost database with current pricing
- Support PM during construction with cost questions
- Review change order pricing

**How AI + software makes this effortless:**
- **AI-Powered Takeoff Assistance** — Upload plans, AI identifies rooms, dimensions, and assemblies. Suggests quantities based on plan analysis and historical projects. "This 3,200 SF coastal home is similar to Smith Residence (3,100 SF, same flood zone). Here's a starting template."
- **Living Cost Database** — Every completed project feeds back actual costs vs. estimated. AI maintains current pricing per unit, per trade, per region. "Framing labor: estimated $12/SF, actual average across last 5 jobs: $13.40/SF. Recommend adjusting."
- **Vendor Bid Comparison** — AI normalizes bids from different formats into apples-to-apples comparison. Highlights scope gaps: "Vendor A bid doesn't include cleanup. Vendor B excludes material handling. Adjusted comparison: A = $45K, B = $42K."
- **Assembly-Based Estimating** — Build reusable assemblies: "Master Bathroom" = tile, fixtures, plumbing rough, electrical, drywall, paint. AI suggests assemblies based on plan room names and adjusts quantities by dimensions.
- **Profitability Feedback Loop** — For every estimate line item, see how actual costs compared on similar past projects. AI highlights items that are consistently over/under estimated.
- **Running Task List** — "1. Complete Johnson takeoff (bid due Friday). 2. Update framing costs from Smith job actuals. 3. Review 3 vendor bids for Wilson electrical. 4. Prepare Harbor View estimate presentation."

**Links to other roles:**
- Provides estimates to → Owner, PM, Client
- Receives plans from → Architect, Client
- Solicits bids from → Vendors/Subs
- Historical data from → PM (job actuals), Bookkeeper (final costs)
- Cost database shared with → PM (for CO pricing), Purchasing
- Estimate templates used by → PM (budget setup on new jobs)

### 6. Bookkeeper / Accountant

**Who they are:** Manages the money. Processes payables, tracks receivables, reconciles accounts, generates financial reports, and ensures the company stays financially healthy.

**Daily duties:**
- Process vendor invoices (match to PO, code to job/cost code, enter in system)
- Issue payments (checks, ACH) on schedule
- Generate and send client invoices / draw requests
- Track accounts receivable and follow up on past-due
- Reconcile bank and credit card statements
- Process payroll (or coordinate with payroll service)
- Manage lien waiver collection and tracking
- Generate financial reports (P&L, balance sheet, job cost reports)
- Handle tax document preparation (1099s, sales tax)
- Coordinate with external CPA for tax filing

**How AI + software makes this effortless:**
- **AI Invoice Processing** — Vendor emails invoice → AI extracts all line items, matches to open PO, codes to correct job and cost code, flags discrepancies. "ABC Electric invoice $12,450 — matches PO-0142 line items within 2%. Auto-coded to Smith Job > 26000-Electrical. Ready for PM approval." Bookkeeper just reviews exceptions.
- **Automated Payment Scheduling** — AI optimizes payment timing: "Pay Jones Plumbing by Friday to capture 2% early-pay discount ($340 savings). Defer ABC Electric to net-30 — cash flow is tight next week." Generates payment batch for approval.
- **Lien Waiver Automation** — Before any payment is issued, system verifies: correct lien waiver type, properly executed, amounts match, notarization if required. AI tracks conditional vs. unconditional and ensures proper sequencing.
- **Two-Way QuickBooks Sync** — Every transaction syncs bidirectionally. No double entry. Conflicts are flagged, not silently overwritten.
- **WIP Report Generator** — AI calculates work-in-progress for every job: % complete (from field), costs to date, estimated cost to complete, over/under billing. Auto-generates the report accountants need.
- **Running Task List** — "1. Process 8 new invoices (3 auto-matched, 5 need review). 2. Follow up on Johnson draw #3 payment (14 days outstanding). 3. Collect lien waiver from Jones Plumbing before Friday payment. 4. Monthly close — 3 items to reconcile."

**Links to other roles:**
- Receives approved invoices from → PM, Owner
- Lien waiver status to → PM (for draw packages), Owner
- Financial reports to → Owner, PM
- Payment status visible to → Vendors (portal), PM
- Payroll data from → Superintendent (time tracking), HR
- Cost data feeds → Estimator (actuals vs. estimates)
- Cash flow data to → Owner (forecasting)
- QuickBooks sync → External CPA

- Verify received materials match POs
- Track backorders and lead times

**How AI + software makes this effortless:**

"Lumber package for Smith job: $34,200 from Atlantic Lumber (best price by 4%, 5-day lead time, in stock)."
- **Price Intelligence** — AI tracks pricing across all vendors and all jobs. "OSB has dropped 8% since your estimate. If you reorder now vs. next month, projected savings: $1,800." Also alerts to price spikes.
- **Delivery Coordination** — Links PO delivery dates to job schedule. AI alerts when a delivery needs to be moved: "Drywall delivery scheduled for Tuesday but framing inspection not until Wednesday. Recommend rescheduling to Thursday to avoid restocking fees."
- **Vendor Scorecard** — AI rates vendors on: price competitiveness, on-time delivery, quality (callback rate), responsiveness. "ABC Lumber: 94% on-time, 2% damage rate, average pricing. Jones Supply: 78% on-time, 1% damage rate, 6% below average pricing."
- **Running Task List** — "1. Issue Wilson framing PO (blocking schedule). 2. Follow up on Smith appliance backorder (8 weeks, check alternatives). 3. Review 3 quotes for Johnson tile. 4. Verify Miller trim delivery for Thursday."

**Links to other roles:**
- PO requests from → PM, Superintendent

- Investigate incidents and near-misses
- Ensure PPE compliance

**How AI + software makes this effortless:**

- **Running Task List** — "1. Conduct weekly inspection at Wilson job. 2. Schedule toolbox talk on ladder safety (3 incidents this quarter). 3. Verify new sub's safety program. 4. Update Smith job safety plan for roofing phase."

**Links to other roles:**

### 9. Warranty / Service Manager

**Who they are:** Handles post-completion warranty claims, coordinates repairs, maintains client relationships after project delivery.

**Daily duties:**
- Receive and log warranty claims from homeowners
- Triage claims (warranty vs. homeowner maintenance vs. manufacturer)
- Schedule and coordinate warranty repairs
- Track manufacturer warranty periods and terms
- Manage vendor callbacks for warranty work
- Conduct 30-day, 6-month, 1-year warranty walkthroughs
- Maintain warranty documentation and close-out packages

**How AI + software makes this effortless:**
- **Client Warranty Portal** — Homeowner submits claim with photos through their portal. AI classifies: "Drywall crack at corner — likely normal settling (95% confidence). Recommend monitoring. If it worsens, schedule drywall repair." vs. "Water stain on ceiling below bathroom — potential plumbing issue. Urgent: schedule inspection within 48 hours."
- **Automated Warranty Scheduling** — AI schedules warranty walkthroughs based on completion date. Auto-generates checklist based on job specifics (coastal = check flashing, elevated = check piling connections).
- **Vendor Callback Tracking** — AI routes warranty items to responsible vendor based on original scope. "Drywall crack → Smith Drywall (original installer). Window leak → Pella (manufacturer warranty, submitted claim #4521)." Tracks response time and resolution.
- **Running Task List** — "1. Schedule 6-month walkthrough for Davis Renovation (due next week). 2. Follow up on Smith window warranty claim (Pella, 10 days open). 3. Close 3 resolved warranty items. 4. Review Miller 30-day walkthrough notes."

**Links to other roles:**
- Claims routed to → Vendors/Subs (callbacks), PM (if major)
- Warranty status to → Client (portal), Owner

## CROSS-ROLE WORKFLOWS (How Everything Connects)

---

### Workflow 1: Invoice → Payment (touches 6 roles)

```
Vendor submits invoice (Vendor Portal)
  → AI extracts, matches to PO, codes to job (System)
  → PM reviews and approves (PM Task List)
  → If over threshold → Owner approves (Owner Approval Queue)
  → Bookkeeper schedules payment (Bookkeeper Task List)
  → System requests lien waiver from vendor (Vendor Portal)
  → Vendor signs lien waiver (Vendor Portal)
  → Payment issued (Bookkeeper)
  → Vendor sees "Paid" status (Vendor Portal)
  → Cost data feeds job budget (PM Dashboard)
  → Cost data feeds estimate database (Estimator)
```

### Workflow 2: Change Order (touches 7 roles)

```
Field condition discovered (Superintendent daily log / photo)
  → AI detects it requires a change order (System)
  → RFI auto-generated to Architect (Architect Portal)
  → Architect responds with solution (Architect Portal)
  → PM creates CO with AI-calculated cost (PM Task List)
  → Client reviews and approves in portal (Client Portal)
  → Owner approves if above threshold (Owner Approval Queue)
  → Budget auto-updated (PM Dashboard, Bookkeeper)
  → Schedule auto-adjusted (PM/Superintendent)
  → Affected subs notified of scope change (Vendor Portal)
  → POs issued for new materials (Purchasing)
  → CO shows on next draw request (Lender Portal)
```

### Workflow 3: Selection → Installation (touches 8 roles)

```
Designer creates selection board (Designer Portal)
  → Client reviews options in portal (Client Portal)
  → AI checks against allowance (System)
  → Client approves selection (Client Portal)
  → If over allowance → CO generated (PM Task List)
  → Spec sheets auto-distributed to trades (Vendor Portal)
  → PO generated to supplier (Purchasing)
  → Delivery scheduled aligned to job schedule (Superintendent)
  → Material received on site (Superintendent confirms)
  → Trade installs per spec (Field Crew task)
  → Photo documentation (Field Crew / Superintendent)
  → Client sees installed selection in portal (Client Portal)
```

### Workflow 4: Draw Request (touches 6 roles)

```
PM initiates draw based on schedule milestone (PM Task List)
  → AI compiles: schedule of values, progress photos, lien waiver status (System)
  → System collects outstanding lien waivers from subs (Vendor Portal)
  → Bookkeeper reviews financial accuracy (Bookkeeper Task List)
  → Owner signs off (Owner Approval Queue)
  → Client approves (if required) (Client Portal)
  → Package submitted to lender (Lender Portal)
  → Lender reviews and approves (Lender Portal)
  → Funds disbursed (Bookkeeper)
  → Payment batch to vendors (Bookkeeper → Vendor Portal)
```

### Workflow 5: Daily Progress Flow (touches 8 roles)

```
Field crew checks in via GPS (Field Crew app)
  → Superintendent walks site, voice logs progress (Superintendent app)
  → AI transcribes, categorizes, tags photos (System)
  → Daily log auto-generated (System)
  → PM sees progress summary in dashboard (PM Dashboard)
  → Schedule auto-updates % complete (PM/Superintendent)
  → Client sees progress photos in portal (Client Portal)
  → Best photos flagged for marketing (Marketing)
  → Labor hours flow to payroll (HR/Bookkeeper)
  → Progress feeds draw request calculations (PM/Bookkeeper)
```

### Workflow 6: New Project Kickoff (touches 10+ roles)

```
Lead converts to contract (Owner/Marketing → PM)
  → PM creates job in system (PM)
  → AI imports estimate as budget baseline (Estimator → PM)
  → Architect uploads final construction documents (Architect Portal)
  → PM builds schedule from template (PM)
  → AI suggests schedule based on similar jobs (System)
  → Designer creates selection schedule with deadlines (Designer Portal)
  → PM sends bid invitations to preferred subs (Vendor Portal)
  → Subs submit bids (Vendor Portal)
  → AI compares bids (System → PM)
  → PM awards scopes, POs generated (PM → Purchasing)
  → Superintendent receives job assignment (Superintendent task list)
  → Client portal activated with project details (Client Portal)
  → Lender receives project setup docs (Lender Portal)
  → Insurance certificates collected (Office Manager)
  → Permits tracked (Office Manager → PM)
```

## THE GOLDEN RULE

> **If someone has to pick up the phone, send an email, or walk to someone's desk to find out the status of something — the software has failed.**

Every role should open RossOS and within 5 seconds know:
1. **What do I need to do right now?** (AI-prioritized task list)
2. **What's blocking me?** (dependency tracking)
3. **What am I blocking?** (downstream awareness)
4. **What changed since I last looked?** (activity feed)
5. **Is anything going wrong?** (AI alerts and risk scoring)
